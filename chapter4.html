A Letter for You

Tap to open

July 2026

Kris,

I've been trying to find the right words, but there aren't enough of them. No sentence is big enough to hold what you mean to me. So I'll try anyway.

I wasn't sure you were even going to show up to that first date. But you did. And somewhere between the nerves and the laughter, something clicked. I walked to my car that night and just sat there for a minute, because I already knew everything was about to change.

I never expected someone like you. Now I can't remember what life was like without you in it. You came in quietly and made everything better. The way you laugh. The way you love. The way you see me, really see me. You surprised me in every single way.

You're consistent. You're steady. You check in on me emotionally without me having to ask. You keep our entire life together with your spreadsheets and lists. You never make me wonder where I stand. You take care of me in ways no one ever has, and you make it look effortless. You show up the same way every single day, and you never waver. That kind of love doesn't just happen. You choose it, over and over.

You are my person.

I love hearing you laugh. It's my favorite sound. I love our nightly check-ins, asking about each other's feelings and favorites. I love that you still love me when I don't feel loveable. I love the way you look at me when you think I'm not paying attention. I love that you never let me doubt us. No matter what, you're there. And I love that you let me love you back.

I used to wonder what my future looked like. Now I just see you. Every morning. Every night. Every ordinary Tuesday that somehow feels extraordinary because you're in it. Growing old together. All of it, with you.

And now I'm asking you to keep choosing me. Forever.

With all my love, always,

Darci
